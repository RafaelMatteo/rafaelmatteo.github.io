  • Recipes
    • From understanding to preparing data
      Dataset description:
      The data was compiled by a researcher named Yong-Yeol Ahn, who extracted tens of thousands of food recipes (cuisines and ingredients) from three different websites, namely:
      • www.allrecipes.com
      • www.epicurious.com
      • www.menupan.com
      Our dataset consists of several recipes and their respective ingredients.
      Each row represents a recipe, and for each recipe, the corresponding geographic area is documented and whether or not the ingredients exist in the recipe, starting with almond and ending with zucchini.
      We want to determine which ingredients are most commonly used in each geographic area.
      For this reason, the name of each recipe was removed, leaving only the geographic area and ingredients.

      • Dataset: recipes.csv
      • Tools: Python, Jupyter Lab, Numpy, Pandas
      • You can download the complete project (zip) or just the code (pdf).

  • Teaching Ratings
    • Evaluating Teacher Productivity and Beauty
      Introduction:
      The data were obtained from end-of-semester evaluations of students in 463 courses taught by a sample of 94 professors at the University of Texas at Austin.
      In addition, six students rated the physical appearance of the professors.
      The result is a data frame where each row contains a different course and each column has information about the course and the professor who taught it.
      • Dataset extracted from: Evaluating Teacher Productivity and Beauty
      • Tools: Python, Jupyter Lab, Numpy, Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn
